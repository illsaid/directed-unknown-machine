# 116 — Keep conditional precedence unresolved without its triggering fact

## Type

real decision handoff

## User

A growth lead delegating a constrained channel-allocation decision to an analyst.

## Situation

Paid acquisition and referral each satisfy independent gates, and only one may be funded. The Success rule gives referral precedence only when a partner-exclusivity clause applies, but the supplied evidence does not establish whether that clause applies.

## Input

Decision: Decide whether to fund paid acquisition or the referral program this quarter; the budget permits only one.
Evidence: The audited contribution margin is 34 percent. The preregistered retained-user conversion rate is 68 percent. Legal review has not established whether the partner-exclusivity clause applies this quarter.
Constraints: Paid acquisition requires contribution margin of at least 30 percent. The referral program requires retained-user conversion of at least 60 percent. Only one program may be funded.
Success: If contribution margin is at least 30 percent, fund paid acquisition. If retained-user conversion is at least 60 percent, fund the referral program. If both branches qualify and the partner-exclusivity clause applies, the referral program takes precedence.

## Expected useful outcome

The compiled contract must preserve both branches as satisfied while leaving branch authority unresolved. The conditional referral precedence must not govern because its triggering fact is unresolved, and the analyst must identify that missing fact rather than choose a program.

## Actual outcome

Run 118 strengthened the recommendation obligation so conditional precedence governs only when every supplied triggering condition is established; an unresolved trigger leaves precedence and branch authority unresolved.

## Whether the system helped

yes

## What broke

The prior obligation applied supplied precedence but did not explicitly say that every condition activating a conditional precedence rule must itself be established.

## What would make the result more useful

Test the same conditional precedence with the triggering fact established and verify that referral governs while paid acquisition remains satisfied but non-governing.
