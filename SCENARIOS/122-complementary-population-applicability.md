# 122 — Resolve a universal trigger across complementary governed populations

## Type

real decision handoff

## User

A growth lead delegating a constrained channel-allocation decision to an analyst.

## Situation

Paid acquisition and referral each satisfy independent gates, and only one may be funded. Two supplied governance rules cover complementary customer populations without gaps. Internal legal governs US enterprise customers and says the exclusivity clause does not apply there. Regional counsel governs every other target customer and says the clause applies there. Referral precedence requires the clause to apply to every target customer.

## Input

Decision: Decide whether to fund paid acquisition or the referral program; the budget permits only one.
Evidence: The audited contribution margin is 34 percent. The preregistered retained-user conversion rate is 68 percent. Internal legal says the partner-exclusivity clause does not apply to US enterprise customers. Regional counsel says the clause applies to every target customer outside the US enterprise segment. The approved governance policy states that internal legal determines operational applicability for US enterprise customers and regional counsel determines operational applicability for all other target customers. Together those populations comprise every target customer.
Constraints: Paid acquisition requires contribution margin of at least 30 percent. The referral program requires retained-user conversion of at least 60 percent. Only one program may be funded.
Success: If contribution margin is at least 30 percent, fund paid acquisition. If retained-user conversion is at least 60 percent, fund the referral program. If both branches qualify and the partner-exclusivity clause applies to every target customer, the referral program takes precedence.

## Expected useful outcome

The compiled contract must evaluate the universal precedence trigger across both complementary governed populations. Because the clause does not apply to US enterprise customers, the every-customer trigger is violated even though it applies to all remaining customers. Referral precedence must not activate; paid acquisition governs while referral remains satisfied but non-governing.

## Actual outcome

Run 124 strengthened the recommendation obligation so complementary applicability scopes are combined without averaging or extrapolation. A universal trigger is satisfied only when every governed scope supports it and is violated when any governed scope rejects it.

## Whether the system helped

yes

## What broke

The prior obligation required every scope to be established but did not explicitly state how a universal trigger resolves when complementary governed scopes jointly cover the whole population and produce different results.

## What would make the result more useful

Test complementary scopes that overlap and supply a rule for which source governs the overlap.
