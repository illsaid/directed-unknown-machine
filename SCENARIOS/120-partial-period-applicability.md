# 120 — Keep partial-period applicability from resolving a full-period trigger

## Type

real decision handoff

## User

A growth lead delegating a constrained quarterly channel-allocation decision to an analyst.

## Situation

Paid acquisition and referral each satisfy independent gates, and only one may be funded. Legal sources conflict about whether a partner-exclusivity clause applies for the quarter. The supplied governance rule makes internal legal authoritative only for the first six weeks, while the precedence trigger requires the clause to apply for the full quarter.

## Input

Decision: Decide whether to fund paid acquisition or the referral program this quarter; the budget permits only one.
Evidence: The audited contribution margin is 34 percent. The preregistered retained-user conversion rate is 68 percent. Outside counsel says the partner-exclusivity clause applies for the full quarter. Internal legal says the clause does not apply during the first six weeks and has not assessed the remaining seven weeks. The approved governance policy states that internal legal determines operational applicability of contract clauses during the first six weeks of each quarter; it gives no governing source for the remaining weeks.
Constraints: Paid acquisition requires contribution margin of at least 30 percent. The referral program requires retained-user conversion of at least 60 percent. Only one program may be funded.
Success: If contribution margin is at least 30 percent, fund paid acquisition. If retained-user conversion is at least 60 percent, fund the referral program. If both branches qualify and the partner-exclusivity clause applies for the full quarter, the referral program takes precedence.

## Expected useful outcome

The compiled contract must apply internal legal only to the first six weeks, preserve the remaining seven weeks as unresolved, keep both candidate branches satisfied, and report precedence unresolved because partial-period applicability cannot establish a full-quarter trigger.

## Actual outcome

Before Run 122, the recommendation obligation said supplied applicability may resolve conflicting trigger evidence but did not require scope matching. An analyst could incorrectly extend the six-week governance rule across the full quarter and activate referral precedence.

## Whether the system helped

partial

## What broke

Applicability authority was not explicitly limited to the period it covers, so a partial-period rule could be overextended to resolve a full-period precedence trigger.

## What would make the result more useful

Require applicability evidence to govern only its explicit scope and keep uncovered trigger periods unresolved.
