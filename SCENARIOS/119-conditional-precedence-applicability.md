# 119 — Preserve displaced trigger evidence when applicability resolves precedence

## Type

real decision handoff

## User

A growth lead delegating a constrained channel-allocation decision to an analyst.

## Situation

Paid acquisition and referral each satisfy independent gates, and only one may be funded. Legal sources conflict about whether a partner-exclusivity clause applies, but the supplied governance rule states that internal legal controls operational applicability for this quarter.

## Input

Decision: Decide whether to fund paid acquisition or the referral program this quarter; the budget permits only one.
Evidence: The audited contribution margin is 34 percent. The preregistered retained-user conversion rate is 68 percent. Outside counsel says the partner-exclusivity clause applies this quarter. Internal legal says the clause does not apply this quarter. The approved governance policy states that internal legal determines operational applicability of contract clauses for quarterly channel funding decisions.
Constraints: Paid acquisition requires contribution margin of at least 30 percent. The referral program requires retained-user conversion of at least 60 percent. Only one program may be funded.
Success: If contribution margin is at least 30 percent, fund paid acquisition. If retained-user conversion is at least 60 percent, fund the referral program. If both branches qualify and the partner-exclusivity clause applies, the referral program takes precedence.

## Expected useful outcome

The compiled contract must apply the supplied governance rule, treat internal legal as governing the precedence trigger, recommend paid acquisition, and preserve outside counsel's conflicting opinion as supplied but non-governing rather than deleting or relabeling it as false.

## Actual outcome

Run 121 strengthened the recommendation obligation so applicability-resolved trigger conflicts identify the governing source while preserving displaced sources as conflicting but non-governing.

## Whether the system helped

yes

## What broke

The prior obligation allowed supplied applicability evidence to resolve which source governs, but did not explicitly require the displaced conflicting source to remain visible. An analyst could correctly choose internal legal while silently erasing outside counsel's contrary opinion.

## What would make the result more useful

Test an applicability rule that governs only part of the trigger period, leaving the remaining period unresolved.
