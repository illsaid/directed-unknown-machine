# 123 — Resolve overlapping governed population scopes

## Type

real decision handoff

## User

A growth lead delegating a constrained channel-allocation decision to an analyst.

## Situation

Paid acquisition and referral each satisfy independent gates, and only one may be funded. Internal legal governs all US customers and says the exclusivity clause does not apply. Regional counsel governs all enterprise customers and says the clause applies. US enterprise customers sit inside both scopes. A supplied governance rule says internal legal controls wherever the scopes overlap. Referral precedence requires the clause to apply to every target customer.

## Input

Decision: Decide whether to fund paid acquisition or the referral program; the budget permits only one.
Evidence: The audited contribution margin is 34 percent. The preregistered retained-user conversion rate is 68 percent. Internal legal says the partner-exclusivity clause does not apply to any US customer. Regional counsel says the clause applies to every enterprise customer. US enterprise customers belong to both populations. The approved governance policy states that internal legal controls operational applicability wherever its population overlaps another legal authority. The supplied population map states that US customers and non-US enterprise customers together comprise every target customer.
Constraints: Paid acquisition requires contribution margin of at least 30 percent. The referral program requires retained-user conversion of at least 60 percent. Only one program may be funded.
Success: If contribution margin is at least 30 percent, fund paid acquisition. If retained-user conversion is at least 60 percent, fund the referral program. If both branches qualify and the partner-exclusivity clause applies to every target customer, the referral program takes precedence.

## Expected useful outcome

The compiled contract must resolve the overlapping US-enterprise population using the supplied overlap-governance rule before evaluating the universal trigger. Internal legal governs the overlap, so the clause does not apply there. The every-customer trigger is therefore violated; referral precedence must not activate. Paid acquisition governs while referral remains satisfied but non-governing, and regional counsel remains visible as displaced only within the overlap.

## Actual outcome

Run 125 strengthened the recommendation obligation so overlapping applicability scopes are partitioned by supplied overlap authority before a universal trigger is evaluated. Displaced evidence remains visible within the overlap rather than being treated as false everywhere.

## Whether the system helped

yes

## What broke

The prior obligation handled complementary scopes but did not explicitly prevent overlapping scopes from being double-counted or treated as an unresolved contradiction even when a supplied rule governed the overlap.

## What would make the result more useful

Test overlapping scopes with no supplied overlap authority; the overlap should remain unresolved rather than inheriting either source.
