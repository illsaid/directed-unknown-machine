# 009 — Vendor renewal decision

## Type

non-editorial-business-transfer

## User

A small-business operator deciding whether to renew an analytics vendor.

## Situation

The annual contract renews next week. The vendor is convenient, but usage has fallen and a cheaper manual workflow may cover the remaining need.

## Input

Decision: Renew the analytics vendor for another year or cancel before the renewal deadline.
Evidence: The team used the vendor in 3 of the last 12 weekly reporting cycles; the annual renewal is $2,400; recreating the three used reports manually took about 45 minutes each in a trial.
Constraints: Do not assign a dollar value to staff time that the operator did not provide; do not assume future usage will match past usage; preserve the cancellation deadline as a real constraint.
Success: Recommend renew or cancel, identify the decisive known fact, and name the unresolved assumption that could reverse the recommendation.

## Expected useful outcome

Preserve a non-editorial commercial decision, historical usage evidence, anti-invention constraints, and a reversible success test within the same four-field contract.

## Actual outcome

Run 10 preserves all four clauses. The fixed brief remains suitable for a vendor renewal decision, and the deliverable now explicitly judges the recommendation against the operator's stated success condition.

## Whether the system helped

yes

## What broke

The prior deliverable requested a recommendation but did not explicitly bind that recommendation to the supplied success condition.

## What would make the result more useful

Test whether a later scenario contains an essential instruction that does not fit Decision, Evidence, Constraints, or Success before adding any field.
