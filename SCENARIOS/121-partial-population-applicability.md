# 121 — Keep majority-population applicability from resolving a full-population trigger

## Type

real decision handoff

## User

A growth lead delegating a constrained channel-allocation decision to an analyst.

## Situation

Paid acquisition and referral each satisfy independent gates, and only one may be funded. Legal sources conflict about whether a partner-exclusivity clause applies to the target customer population. The supplied governance rule makes internal legal authoritative only for US enterprise customers, who represent 80 percent of the target population, while the precedence trigger requires the clause to apply to every target customer.

## Input

Decision: Decide whether to fund paid acquisition or the referral program; the budget permits only one.
Evidence: The audited contribution margin is 34 percent. The preregistered retained-user conversion rate is 68 percent. Outside counsel says the partner-exclusivity clause applies to every target customer. Internal legal says the clause does not apply to US enterprise customers and has not assessed other target customers. The approved governance policy states that internal legal determines operational applicability for US enterprise customers only. US enterprise customers represent 80 percent of the target population.
Constraints: Paid acquisition requires contribution margin of at least 30 percent. The referral program requires retained-user conversion of at least 60 percent. Only one program may be funded.
Success: If contribution margin is at least 30 percent, fund paid acquisition. If retained-user conversion is at least 60 percent, fund the referral program. If both branches qualify and the partner-exclusivity clause applies to every target customer, the referral program takes precedence.

## Expected useful outcome

The compiled contract must apply internal legal only to US enterprise customers, preserve the remaining target customers as unresolved, keep both candidate branches satisfied, and report precedence unresolved because authority over 80 percent of the population cannot establish a trigger requiring every target customer.

## Actual outcome

Run 123 strengthened the recommendation obligation so a covered majority cannot stand in for an uncovered remainder. US enterprise customers are resolved under internal legal, other target customers remain unresolved, and the full-population precedence trigger cannot activate.

## Whether the system helped

yes

## What broke

The prior obligation limited applicability to its explicit population scope, but did not explicitly prohibit treating a covered majority as representative of the uncovered population.

## What would make the result more useful

Test complementary population-scoped applicability rules that jointly cover the full target population without gaps.
