# 124 — Preserve unresolved authority inside overlapping scopes

## Type

real decision handoff

## User

A growth lead delegating a constrained channel-allocation decision to an analyst.

## Situation

Paid acquisition and referral each satisfy independent gates, and only one may be funded. Internal legal governs all US customers and says the exclusivity clause does not apply. Regional counsel governs all enterprise customers and says it applies. US enterprise customers sit inside both scopes, but no supplied policy says which authority governs the overlap. Referral precedence requires the clause to apply to every target customer.

## Input

Decision: Decide whether to fund paid acquisition or the referral program; the budget permits only one.
Evidence: The audited contribution margin is 34 percent. The preregistered retained-user conversion rate is 68 percent. Internal legal says the partner-exclusivity clause does not apply to any US customer. Regional counsel says the clause applies to every enterprise customer. US enterprise customers belong to both populations. No supplied governance policy establishes which legal authority controls inside the overlap. The supplied population map states that US customers and non-US enterprise customers together comprise every target customer.
Constraints: Paid acquisition requires contribution margin of at least 30 percent. The referral program requires retained-user conversion of at least 60 percent. Only one program may be funded.
Success: If contribution margin is at least 30 percent, fund paid acquisition. If retained-user conversion is at least 60 percent, fund the referral program. If both branches qualify and the partner-exclusivity clause applies to every target customer, the referral program takes precedence.

## Expected useful outcome

The compiled contract must keep clause applicability unresolved for US enterprise customers because the supplied authorities conflict inside the overlap and no overlap-governance rule resolves them. Referral precedence therefore cannot activate. Both channel branches remain satisfied, but branch authority remains unresolved because only one may be funded and no governing precedence is established.

## Actual outcome

Run 126 strengthened the recommendation obligation so unresolved authority inside an overlap remains explicit and blocks any broader trigger that depends on that overlap. Neither source may be inherited by convenience.

## Whether the system helped

yes

## What broke

The prior obligation explained how to apply supplied overlap authority, but did not explicitly state the refusal behavior when no overlap authority exists. An analyst could still select either source inside the shared population and manufacture a resolved universal trigger.

## What would make the result more useful

Stop extending population-scope permutations. Test the six audit obligations against a fresh decision handoff from a different domain.
