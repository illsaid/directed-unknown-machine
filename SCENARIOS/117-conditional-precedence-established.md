# 117 — Apply conditional precedence only with its triggering fact established

## Type

real decision handoff

## User

A growth lead delegating a constrained channel-allocation decision to an analyst.

## Situation

Paid acquisition and referral each satisfy independent gates, and only one may be funded. The Success rule gives referral precedence when a partner-exclusivity clause applies, and the supplied legal evidence establishes that the clause applies this quarter.

## Input

Decision: Decide whether to fund paid acquisition or the referral program this quarter; the budget permits only one.
Evidence: The audited contribution margin is 34 percent. The preregistered retained-user conversion rate is 68 percent. Legal review confirms that the partner-exclusivity clause applies this quarter.
Constraints: Paid acquisition requires contribution margin of at least 30 percent. The referral program requires retained-user conversion of at least 60 percent. Only one program may be funded.
Success: If contribution margin is at least 30 percent, fund paid acquisition. If retained-user conversion is at least 60 percent, fund the referral program. If both branches qualify and the partner-exclusivity clause applies, the referral program takes precedence.

## Expected useful outcome

The compiled contract must select referral as the governing branch, cite the legal evidence that activates conditional precedence, and preserve paid acquisition as satisfied but non-governing rather than failed or omitted.

## Actual outcome

Run 119 strengthened the recommendation obligation so activated conditional precedence must cite its triggering evidence while preserving displaced satisfied branches.

## Whether the system helped

yes

## What broke

The prior obligation required conditional precedence triggers to be established, but once established it did not explicitly require the recommendation to cite the evidence that activated precedence.

## What would make the result more useful

Test conflicting supplied evidence about the precedence trigger; conditional precedence should remain unresolved rather than selecting whichever source is convenient.
