# 118 — Keep conditional precedence unresolved under conflicting trigger evidence

## Type

real decision handoff

## User

A growth lead delegating a constrained channel-allocation decision to an analyst.

## Situation

Paid acquisition and referral each satisfy independent gates, and only one may be funded. Referral takes precedence when a partner-exclusivity clause applies, but supplied legal sources conflict about whether that clause applies this quarter.

## Input

Decision: Decide whether to fund paid acquisition or the referral program this quarter; the budget permits only one.
Evidence: The audited contribution margin is 34 percent. The preregistered retained-user conversion rate is 68 percent. Outside counsel says the partner-exclusivity clause applies this quarter. Internal legal says the clause does not apply this quarter. No supplied evidence establishes which legal opinion governs.
Constraints: Paid acquisition requires contribution margin of at least 30 percent. The referral program requires retained-user conversion of at least 60 percent. Only one program may be funded.
Success: If contribution margin is at least 30 percent, fund paid acquisition. If retained-user conversion is at least 60 percent, fund the referral program. If both branches qualify and the partner-exclusivity clause applies, the referral program takes precedence.

## Expected useful outcome

The compiled contract must preserve both satisfied candidate branches, keep conditional precedence and branch authority unresolved because the trigger evidence conflicts, and identify the missing applicability rule rather than selecting the convenient legal source.

## Actual outcome

Run 120 strengthened the recommendation obligation so conflicting supplied evidence about a conditional-precedence trigger cannot activate precedence unless supplied applicability evidence resolves which source governs.

## Whether the system helped

yes

## What broke

The prior obligation required a trigger to be established and cited, but did not explicitly say that conflicting supplied evidence leaves the trigger unresolved. An analyst could cite only the favorable legal opinion and activate referral precedence.

## What would make the result more useful

Test the same conflict with a supplied applicability rule that establishes which legal source governs.
